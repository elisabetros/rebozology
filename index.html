What is a Rebozo?

The rebozo, or traditional Mexican shawl used by women for centuries for different purposes, can help the future mother confront the challenge of birth with greater comfort, confidence and security, it's a natural pain relief during pregnancy and birth.

Continued use of the rebozo after birth represents an on-going connection between mother and child. While the child is wrapped in the rebozo, s/he has physical contact with the mother’s body, also used by fathers. The baby sleeps, is breastfed and otherwise socialized in this marvelous nest.

After birth the “Closing Ceremony” with Rebozo and herbs bath gives new mothers the physical and emotional healing they need for postpartum.

The rebozo follows the woman's journey through life starting with pregnancy and ending as shawl and scarf that you can wear the rest of your life and holds all the good memories between you and your baby.

Why to use a Rebozo?

During Pregnancy, Birth and Postpartum

Practicality

Benefits

The rebozo is a natural pain relief, used in Mexico for hundreds of years, that provides comfort to pregnant women when in pain or discomfort, as well as to help the baby into the most appropriate position for an easy birth.
Easy to use - These rebozo techniques can be used right away with your birth partner or they can also be taught by trained Doulas or Midwifes. (For complicated pregnancies, consult with your midwife). It’s a great way of involving your partner since the beginning.

As a Baby Carrier

  • Optimal support for the babies' head and spine
  • Babies that are in contact with the body of their mother/father and are therefore more comfortable, calm and safe.
  • Going belly-to-belly in an upright position helps the digestive system and reduces colic
  • By staying at the same height as mom/dad/partner the baby is easily integrated into social life because all communication includes them
  • Babies sleep better when they are in contact with the mother/father/partner
  • Safety, it has been used in the Mexican culture through centuries
  • Carry a new born through toddlerhood (0-5) in the same rebozo, they are built to last
  • Easy to use, one easy slip-knot and you’re done
  • Transfer your baby to their nap more smoothly – lay them down easily straight from the rebozo
  • Transparent – baby can feel secure by seeing out and reducing over stimulation
  • Hands free – whether breastfeeding or bottle-feeding
  • Healthier for your body, distributes the weight of the baby
  • Helps reinforce the relationship between the baby and it's caretakers
